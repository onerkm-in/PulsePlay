# CPG-FMCG Glossary

Alphabetical industry vocabulary. Each entry is a one-sentence definition with the source standard or industry practice cited where applicable.

## A

**Accrual (trade)** — A forward-recognised liability for trade-promotion spend that has been committed to a customer but not yet paid out (rebates, off-invoice deals, scan-backs, MDF). Source: standard CPG finance practice; tracked in TPM systems.

**Allergens (manufacturing)** — Substances that require segregated handling, dedicated lines or thorough changeover sanitation to prevent cross-contamination (peanut, milk, gluten, soy, egg, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, sesame). Source: regulatory; in the US tracked under the Food Allergen Labeling and Consumer Protection Act (FALCPA).

**Assortment** — The set of SKUs offered to a given customer, channel, or store cluster. Source: standard category-management practice.

## B

**Batch** — A discrete production run identifiable by a batch/lot number, used for quality, recall, and traceability. Source: GS1 traceability practice; aligns with GS1 GTIN + batch/lot AI (10).

**BOM (Bill of Materials)** — The structured list of raw materials, ingredients, packaging, and intermediate products that go into a finished good. Source: ERP / PLM standard practice.

**BRCGS** — Brand Reputation Compliance Global Standards; food-safety certification scheme benchmarked under GFSI. Source: BRCGS (https://www.brcgs.com/).

## C

**Case fill rate** — Percentage of cases ordered that were shipped on the original line. Compare to OTIF and order fill rate. Source: standard CPG supply chain KPI.

**Category captain** — The supplier a retailer designates to lead category-management decisions (assortment, planogram, promo) for a category at that retailer. Source: standard retail-category-management practice.

**CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive)** — EU directive requiring large companies to report sustainability information using European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS). Source: EU Commission (https://finance.ec.europa.eu/capital-markets-union-and-financial-markets/company-reporting-and-auditing/company-reporting/corporate-sustainability-reporting_en).

**Cost-to-serve** — The fully-loaded cost (manufacturing, logistics, fulfilment, customer service, returns, deductions) of serving a specific customer, channel, or order pattern. Source: standard managerial-accounting practice.

## D

**DC (Distribution Centre)** — A warehouse holding finished goods for onward distribution to customers. Source: standard logistics terminology.

**Deduction** — A short-pay applied by a customer (typically a retailer) against an invoice, citing reasons like price discrepancy, shortage, damage, or promo non-compliance. Source: standard CPG finance practice.

**Digital shelf** — The aggregated retailer-website / marketplace presence of a brand: content, ratings, reviews, search rank, availability, price parity, competitor presence. Source: digital-shelf-analytics industry practice.

## E

**EAN/UPC** — European Article Number / Universal Product Code; barcode encoding of a GTIN. Source: GS1 General Specifications (https://www.gs1.org/standards/barcodes).

**ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance)** — Reporting category covering environmental impact, social responsibility, and governance practices. Source: investor and reporting framework practice; multiple standards (GRI, SASB, TCFD).

**ESRS** — European Sustainability Reporting Standards; the standards content under CSRD. Source: EFRAG (https://www.efrag.org/).

## F

**Fill rate** — Percentage of demand satisfied directly from available inventory at first attempt. Variants: case fill rate, line fill rate, unit fill rate, value fill rate. Source: standard CPG supply chain KPI.

**Forecast accuracy** — Typically MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) or 1-WMAPE (1 minus Weighted MAPE) of demand forecast vs actual at SKU-location-week granularity. Source: standard demand-planning practice; APICS / ASCM body of knowledge.

**FSSC 22000** — Food Safety System Certification 22000; GFSI-benchmarked food-safety scheme. Source: FSSC (https://www.fssc.com/).

## G

**GFSI (Global Food Safety Initiative)** — Industry-driven body that benchmarks food-safety certification schemes (FSSC 22000, BRCGS, SQF, etc.). Source: GFSI (https://mygfsi.com/).

**GHG Protocol** — Greenhouse Gas Protocol; the most widely used standard for corporate emissions accounting (Scope 1, 2, 3). Source: WRI/WBCSD (https://ghgprotocol.org/).

**GLN (Global Location Number)** — GS1 identifier for a physical or functional location (plant, DC, ship-to, store). Source: GS1 General Specifications.

**Gross to Net (GtN)** — The bridge from gross sales to net sales after deducting trade discounts, allowances, returns, and other revenue offsets. Source: standard CPG finance practice.

**GRI (Global Reporting Initiative)** — Sustainability reporting standards body. Source: GRI (https://www.globalreporting.org/standards/).

**GTIN (Global Trade Item Number)** — GS1 unique identifier for a tradable item (consumer unit, case, pallet variant). Source: GS1 General Specifications.

## H

**HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points)** — Preventive food-safety methodology that identifies hazards and critical control points throughout production. Source: Codex Alimentarius (https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/en/).

**Halo effect** — Lift in non-promoted SKUs caused by a promotion on a related SKU. Source: marketing-mix-modelling practice.

## I

**IFRS S2** — International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB) standard on climate-related financial disclosures, evolved from TCFD recommendations. Source: IFRS Foundation (https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/ifrs-sustainability-standards-navigator/ifrs-s2-climate-related-disclosures/).

**Incrementality** — The lift in sales attributable to a marketing or promotional intervention above what would have happened anyway. Source: marketing-effectiveness practice.

**Inventory days** — Inventory on hand divided by average daily cost of goods sold; a working-capital efficiency measure. Source: standard finance / supply chain KPI.

**ISA-95 / IEC 62264** — Standard for enterprise-control system integration; defines manufacturing levels (Level 0 sensors, Level 4 ERP) and exchange models. Source: ISA (https://www.isa.org/standards-and-publications/isa-standards/isa-95-standard).

**ISO 22400** — ISO standard defining manufacturing operations management KPIs (OEE, throughput, scrap rate, etc.). Source: ISO (https://www.iso.org/standard/56847.html).

## J

**JBP (Joint Business Plan)** — Annual or multi-year aligned plan between a CPG manufacturer and a retailer covering volume, growth, promo, innovation, supply chain, and margin commitments. Source: standard retail-collaboration practice.

## L

**Line fill rate** — Percentage of order lines shipped complete. Source: standard supply chain KPI.

## M

**MAPE / WMAPE** — Mean Absolute Percentage Error / Weighted MAPE; common forecast-accuracy metrics. Source: standard demand-planning practice.

**MES (Manufacturing Execution System)** — Plant-floor system that manages production execution between ERP (Level 4) and shop-floor controls (Level 2). Source: ISA-95.

**MRO** — Maintenance, Repair, and Operations supplies (spare parts, lubricants, cleaning supplies, etc.). Source: standard procurement category.

## N

**NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** — Revenue retained from existing customers including expansion, renewals, and contraction; widely used in B2B/SaaS but increasingly applied to enterprise CPG account portfolios. Source: SaaS-metric origin; common B2B usage.

**Net sales** — Gross sales minus revenue deductions (trade, returns, allowances). Source: standard finance definition.

## O

**OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)** — Availability x Performance x Quality, expressed as a percentage of theoretical maximum output for a line. Source: TPM (Total Productive Maintenance) practice; codified in ISO 22400.

**Ontology** — Formal model of entities and relationships in a domain. Source: knowledge-management / semantic-web practice.

**OTIF (On-Time In-Full)** — Percentage of orders delivered both on time and complete to the customer-defined window. Increasingly enforced with retailer-imposed fines for misses. Source: standard CPG supply chain KPI; widely used by Walmart, Target, and other large retailers.

## P

**Phantom inventory** — System-recorded stock that is not actually on the shelf or in the location, typically due to shrinkage, miscount, or misplacement. Source: retail inventory management practice.

**Planogram** — A diagram specifying SKU placement on a shelf or fixture. Source: standard retail merchandising practice.

**PLM (Product Lifecycle Management)** — System and process covering product design, formulation, packaging, regulatory data, and lifecycle management. Source: standard product-development practice.

**Promo ROI** — Incremental margin generated by a promotion divided by promotional spend. Source: standard marketing-effectiveness practice.

## Q

**QM (Quality Management)** — Module / system covering inspection plans, deviations, CAPA, batch release, and audit. Source: standard manufacturing-quality practice.

## R

**Recipe** — Manufacturing master defining ingredients, quantities, and process parameters for a product. Source: standard MES / PLM practice.

**Returns and Allowances** — Deductions from gross sales for product returns and customer-allowed adjustments. Source: standard finance definition.

**RGM (Revenue Growth Management)** — CPG capability covering pricing, promo, mix, pack architecture, and trade investment to maximise profitable revenue. Source: industry-standard commercial discipline; covered in McKinsey, Bain, BCG CPG practice notes.

## S

**SASB Standards** — Sustainability Accounting Standards Board industry-specific materiality standards, now part of IFRS Foundation. Source: SASB / IFRS (https://sasb.ifrs.org/standards/).

**SBTi (Science Based Targets initiative)** — Joint initiative validating corporate emissions-reduction targets against climate science. Source: SBTi (https://sciencebasedtargets.org/).

**Scope 1 emissions** — Direct emissions from owned or controlled sources (combustion in plants, fleet vehicles). Source: GHG Protocol Corporate Standard.

**Scope 2 emissions** — Indirect emissions from purchased electricity, steam, heating, and cooling. Source: GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance.

**Scope 3 emissions** — All other indirect emissions in the value chain (purchased goods and services, transport, business travel, end-of-life, use phase, etc.) — typically the largest emissions category for CPG. Source: GHG Protocol Corporate Value Chain (Scope 3) Standard.

**Service level** — Percentage of demand satisfied within a customer-defined window. Often used interchangeably with case fill rate or OTIF in CPG context. Source: standard CPG supply chain KPI.

**SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)** — Smallest unique product identifier carried in inventory and master data. Source: standard inventory-management practice.

**S&OP (Sales and Operations Planning)** — Cross-functional monthly process aligning demand, supply, finance, and commercial plans. Source: APICS / ASCM body of knowledge.

**SQF** — Safe Quality Food; GFSI-benchmarked food-safety certification scheme. Source: SQFI (https://www.sqfi.com/).

**SSCC (Serial Shipping Container Code)** — GS1 unique identifier for a logistics unit (pallet, container). Source: GS1 General Specifications.

## T

**TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures)** — Recommendations framework for climate-related financial risk disclosure; now consolidated into ISSB IFRS S2. Source: FSB / TCFD (https://www.fsb-tcfd.org/recommendations/).

**TMS (Transportation Management System)** — System managing carriers, lanes, loads, and freight execution. Source: standard logistics practice.

**TPM (Trade Promotion Management)** — System covering planning, execution, settlement, and post-event analysis of trade promotions. Source: standard CPG commercial practice.

**Trade spend** — Investments paid by a CPG manufacturer to retailers and distributors to drive volume, distribution, or visibility (off-invoice allowances, scan-backs, MDF, slotting). Source: standard CPG finance and commercial practice.

## V

**VMI (Vendor Managed Inventory)** — Arrangement where the supplier manages inventory levels at the customer's site/DC. Source: standard supply chain collaboration practice.

## W

**Waste (manufacturing)** — Material lost to scrap, rework, expiry, contamination, or quality reject. Source: standard manufacturing-loss accounting; aligns with TPM "six big losses" framework.

**WMS (Warehouse Management System)** — System managing inbound, putaway, picking, packing, and outbound in a DC. Source: standard logistics practice.

**Working capital** — Current assets minus current liabilities; in CPG primarily inventory + receivables - payables. Source: standard finance definition.
